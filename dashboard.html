Spring for Super Seeds

What are Super Seeds? Super seeds contain nutrients, including vitamins and minerals that are vital to developing and sustaining your health, lower blood pressure, reduce cholesterol and diminish other precursors to chronic health conditions, such as arthritis.

Types of Super Seeds

  • Flaxseeds
  • Pumpkin Seeds
  • Sunflower Seeds
  • Sesame Seeds
  • Chia Seeds

What are the serving sizes of these super seeds? The National Institutes of Health (NIH) recommend four to five half ounce servings of seeds (the great thing is you can select which seeds you want to eat) each week.
Sprinkle these seeds on your salads, bagels or marinades- or just eat them by the handful!

Attack Allergies Naturally

Anti-inflammatory foods are a great way to nip allergy symptoms in the bud!

Here’s a list of the foods you can try- and great recipe ideas to use- to keep ahead of the pollen.

Lightly cooked dark, leafy greens

  • Serving Size: 8-10 cups per day
  • Try with... Eat kale, collard greens or chard sprinkled with sesame seeds

Healthy Fats including avocado or heart-healthy oils such as olive or canola oil

  • Serving Size: 5-7 servings per day
  • Try with... Use olive oil as a dressing with a little vinegar and chop up some avocado to use as a healthy spread on toast

Garlic, turmeric, curry and ginger

  • Serving Size: Unlimited!
